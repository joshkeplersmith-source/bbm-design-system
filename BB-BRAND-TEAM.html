Typography Rules

DO

  • Upright EB Garamond always leads in headings — italic always follows, never opens
  • Use Playfair Display for subheadings, captions, buttons, UI labels, eyebrows, ghosted text
  • Use Work Sans 300 for all body copy; Work Sans 600 for bold emphasis sparingly
  • Ghosted Playfair Display italic at 8–12% opacity on N-100 sections only, once per page

DON'T

  • Open a heading with the italic span
  • Use EB Garamond outside headings and card titles
  • Use Work Sans for headings, subheadings, or UI labels
  • Use uppercase on EB Garamond
  • Mix heading fonts on a single surface

13 — Photography Brief
One Mode · One Visual World
All BBM photography uses a single visual standard: Kodak Vision3 500T / 16mm film emulation. Warm, organic, documentary. Never produced.
Primary Mode — Kodak Vision3 500T / 16mm
AttributeSpecification
Film stockKodak Vision3 500T (7219) — 16mm emulation. Warm but not orange. Cream and sand tones.
SubjectsWomen 30s–50s unposed and real. Men 40s–60s natural energy. Skin texture visible and unhidden.
LightingNatural window light only. Soft highlight halation. Nothing airbrushed or studio-lit.
EditingLifted shadows — nothing goes fully black. Slightly underexposed and organic. Film latitude, not digital precision.
MoodDocumentary, not produced. Lived-in. Real. Unhurried.
ColorMuted saturation. Warm neutrals — cream, sand, soft earth. No bright saturated color.
Image Generation Prompt — 500T Core String
"Give this photo a warm leaning to neutral (avoid anything too sepia toned) Kodak 7219 500T - 16mm - film stock emulation, with some film grain and texture. Preserve the subject framing. No text, no film edges."

16 — Framing Principles
Six Principles That Govern All BBM Content
Architecture rules — apply before the first word is written.
Principle 01
Lead with the feeling, not the treatment.
"Tired but can't sleep" leads. "Hormone pellet therapy" follows. Never reverse this in an ad or email subject line.
Principle 02
Restoration, not transformation.
"Feel like yourself again" is always correct. "Become your best self" is always wrong. The frame is return, not upgrade.
Principle 03
Medical authority, human delivery.
Clinically grounded, warmly delivered. Write like a trusted clinician talking to a patient they know — not like a pharma insert or a lifestyle blog.
Principle 04
Candidacy, not urgency.
CTAs invite qualification — "See if you're a candidate" — not manufactured urgency — "Limited spots!" Scarcity messaging is banned unless literally true and confirmed by Mia or Teresa.
Principle 05
Educate before you sell.
Organic content is educational first. Myth-busting, explainers, and patient questions are the primary mode. Organic posts should almost never read as ads.
Principle 06
The two providers are the brand.
Content should consistently reflect that this is a two-NP practice with overlapping specialties by design. Surface both names and credentials whenever the format allows.
17 — Service Line Compliance
Regulatory Exposure by Service
GLP-1 is the highest-risk line. Hormone therapy has evolving FDA context. Apply all rules before every output.
Non-negotiable rule: If a piece of content might violate a rule below, flag it and ask before submitting.
GLP-1 / Medical Weight Loss — Strictest Rules
RequiredBanned
  • Generic names only: Compounded Semaglutide · Compounded Tirzepatide
  • "Medically supervised" in any ad claiming weight loss results
  • Avoid specific weight-loss outcome claims (lbs lost, % body weight)
  • "Results vary" or "individual results may vary" with testimonials
  • LegitScript Certified as credibility anchor in paid media
  • Ozempic · Wegovy · Mounjaro · Zepbound (brand names)
  • "Lose X lbs in Y weeks" guaranteed outcomes
  • "Miracle" · "instant" · "effortless" · "quick fix"
  • Before/after photos without "results vary" disclaimer
Hormone Optimization — Evolving FDA Context
RequiredBanned
  • "Biote Hormone Pellet Therapy" — always full name with Biote branding
  • "Biote-Certified" as credential for Mia and Teresa
  • TRT: may now state FDA removed cardiovascular black box warning (Feb 2025, TRAVERSE trial)
  • "Medically supervised" for all hormone protocols
  • HRT / Hormone Replacement Therapy (legacy medical term)
  • Claiming hormone therapy cures or reverses specific diseases
  • Implying pellets are FDA-approved drugs (they are compounded)
  • Anti-aging framing in hormone context
All Services — Universal Rules
  • Never claim to diagnose or cure any medical condition
  • Never promise guaranteed outcomes — "results may vary" applies everywhere
  • CareCredit and Cherry Financing must be named providers — not vague "financing available"
  • All copy must be consistent with what BBM's providers have reviewed and approved

FAQ Copy

22 — Graceful Degradation
Two States · Degrade Predictably
When full brand fidelity cannot be achieved, degrade predictably — not randomly. Always invoke the nearest compliant state.
State 1 — Partial Brand
Preferred fallback
  • EB Garamond for headings — substitute Georgia if not loading
  • Work Sans 300 for body — substitute system-ui sans
  • Full color tokens maintained
  • Textures dropped if SVG fails to load — background color holds
  • Provider photos: onerror fallback to neutral placeholder if R2 unreachable
State 2 — Minimal Brand
Last-resort only
  • System fonts throughout
  • BU-700 (#2b3a4a) and N-100 (#f1f0ee) maintained — non-negotiable minimums
  • All textures, shadows, and gradients dropped
  • Naming conventions and compliance rules still fully enforced
  • Never use brand-incorrect colors (no generic blue, no white/black-only)
Degradation is always visual — never a degradation of voice, naming, or compliance rules.
