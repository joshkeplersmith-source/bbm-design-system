Gradient Selection Rule — Content-Coded, Not Decorative

  • Neutral — default for all content without a gender-coded subject. Use when in doubt.

  • Male (GR-family) — male-forward services only: testosterone, peptides, men's metabolic content. Never use for visual variety on gender-neutral content.

Using all three families in a single row requires three tiles each with a distinct gender-coded subject. It is not a layout default. If the tile content does not have a clear gender-coded subject, use Neutral.

16 — Framing Principles
Six Principles That Govern All BBM Content
Architecture rules — apply before the first word is written.
Principle 01
Lead with the feeling, not the treatment.
"Tired but can't sleep" leads. "Hormone pellet therapy" follows. Never reverse this in an ad or email subject line.
Principle 02
Restoration, not transformation.
"Feel like yourself again" is always correct. "Become your best self" is always wrong. The frame is return, not upgrade.
Principle 03
Medical authority, human delivery.
Clinically grounded, warmly delivered. Write like a trusted clinician talking to a patient they know — not like a pharma insert or a lifestyle blog.
Principle 04
Candidacy, not urgency.
CTAs invite qualification — "See if you're a candidate" — not manufactured urgency — "Limited spots!" Scarcity messaging is banned unless literally true and confirmed by Mia or Teresa.
Principle 05
Educate before you sell.
Organic content is educational first. Myth-busting, explainers, and patient questions are the primary mode. Organic posts should almost never read as ads.
Principle 06
The two providers are the brand.
Content should consistently reflect that this is a two-NP practice with overlapping specialties by design. Surface both names and credentials whenever the format allows.

GLP-1 is the highest-risk line. Hormone therapy has evolving FDA context. Apply all rules before every output.
Non-negotiable rule: If a piece of content might violate a rule below, flag it and ask before submitting.
GLP-1 / Medical Weight Loss — Strictest Rules
RequiredBanned

  • "Medically supervised" in any ad claiming weight loss results
  • Avoid specific weight-loss outcome claims (lbs lost, % body weight)
  • "Results vary" or "individual results may vary" with testimonials

  • Before/after photos without "results vary" disclaimer

RequiredBanned

  • "Biote-Certified" as credential for Mia and Teresa

  • HRT / Hormone Replacement Therapy (legacy medical term)

  • Implying pellets are FDA-approved drugs (they are compounded)
  • Anti-aging framing in hormone context

18 — Format Defaults
Channel Format Rules
Apply these before any output is considered complete.
Email
  • Subject line: 40 chars max (mobile preview cutoff)
  • Preview text: 85–100 chars — never blank
  • Single CTA per email — never two competing CTAs
  • CTA label must match Naming Conventions table
  • Em-dash: space — em-dash — space (not hyphen)
  • Sentence-case subject lines only — not Title Case
Meta / Paid Ads
  • Primary text: 125 chars before truncation on mobile
  • Headline: 40 chars max
  • Description: 30 chars max
  • Lead with symptom/feeling — never service name
  • LegitScript badge mention in GLP-1 / weight loss ads
  • No exclamation marks in headlines
FAQ Copy
  • Question: patient voice ("Does it hurt?")
  • Answer: clinician voice — warm, direct, no hedging
  • Max 3 sentences per FAQ answer — if longer, break into bullets
  • Never guarantee outcomes in FAQ answers
Punctuation
  • Em-dash: space — em-dash — space. Never hyphen.
  • Apostrophes: curly ('not ')
  • Numbers: spell out one–nine; numerals for 10+
  • Percent: 80% not 80 percent
