11 — Texture System
Section Backgrounds & Watermarks
Section SVGs (NV-200 fill, #deddd3) add depth and break up long N-100 pages. Deployed rotated 90°, filling the full section as a background layer. N-100 sections only — never on P-700. Logo mark PNG used as a large scale watermark at 5–10% opacity, once per page maximum.
Live Demos · Full-bleed texture fills
Texture 08 · Horizontal wave · 22% opacity · Full bleed
Section content sits above
The NV-200 fill creates a subtle warm contrast against N-100. The texture fills the full section — it doesn't sit in a container. All content layers above it at z-index 1.
Texture 04 · Scattered organic forms · 18% opacity · Full bleed
Different texture, same rule
Each texture SVG has a distinct character. Vary across sections for rhythm. Never use the same texture in two adjacent sections. One texture per section maximum.
Texture 03 · Flowing ribbon · 16% opacity · Full bleed
Opacity varies by texture complexity
More complex, denser textures use lower opacity (14–18%). Simpler forms can go slightly higher (20–26%). Find the level where the texture reads as atmosphere, not pattern.

12 — Photography Brief
Two Modes · One Visual World
Black and white is the primary brand photography mode. Kodak Portra 400 color is the secondary mode. Both serve the same emotional register: real, warm, unhurried, authentic.
Primary Mode — Black & White
"Real women in their late 30s to mid 40s, unposed and natural, photographed with visible skin texture — no smoothing, no airbrushing. Natural window light or soft daylight, warm tones with slight grain or film simulation. Intimate, quiet moments — at ease, not performed. Warm cream, soft linen, or muted warm-gray settings. No studio backdrops. Cinematic, slightly underexposed. Slow and unhurried."
AttributeSpecification
SubjectsReal women late 30s–mid 40s. Diverse. No heavy makeup or styled hair. Skin texture and fine lines are intentional.
Men40s–60s. Natural energy and genuine strength. Real smile, real body. Not performative.
LightingSoft natural window light or golden hour. Warm and directional with shadow presence. No flat fill.
EditingFilm simulation or film-forward digital. Warm grain. Lifted shadows. Soft restrained contrast.
MoodExhale. Quiet relief. Coming back to yourself. Unhurried. Real.
CompositionClose-medium framing. Intimate. Environmental context visible but secondary.
Secondary Mode — Kodak Portra 400 Color
"Portra 400 film stock reference. Soft grain, warm skin tones, natural light. Approachable and real — not clinical, not influencer-coded. Same subjects and emotional register as the B&W work, expressed with warmth through color."
AttributeSpecification
Film referenceKodak Portra 400 — warm tones, fine grain, lifted blacks, natural skin rendering
LightingNatural or practical light. Warm and directional. No harsh flash.
Color in frameWarm neutrals — cream, linen, warm gray, muted earth. Nothing competing with subject.

13 — Do / Don't Rules
Six Categories
Rules for all agents, designers, and team members producing content for Body Balance Medical.
Logo

DO

  • Use Logo-W on P-700, gradient, or photo backgrounds
  • Use Logo-Bu on N-100 or NV backgrounds — preferred light version
  • Use Logo-G sparingly on dark hero moments only
  • Maintain clearspace equal to the cap height of the "b" mark on all sides
  • Observe minimum sizes: H-Logo 120px · V-Logo 80px · Mark 32px
  • Use mark alone only for social avatars and tight spaces

DON'T

  • Place logo on any mid-tone background without sufficient contrast
  • Apply color, shadow, stroke, or effect not in approved four variants
  • Scale below minimum sizes — wordmark never below ~11px
  • Crop, rotate, or distort the logo in any way
  • Place on a busy photographic background without contrast
Color

DO

  • Anchor every page with N-100 as the base page background
  • Use P-700 for section-level hierarchy — section and tile level only
  • Use texture SVGs (NV-200) to add depth to N-100 sections
  • Use Q-300 on dark and Q-700 on light — non-interchangeable
  • Use S-family for female-forward, T-family for male-forward — intentionally
  • Use NV-500 and NV-700 for all dividers, rules, underlines, callout lines

DON'T

  • Use mid-tones (300–600 from any family) as section or page backgrounds
  • Use P-700 as a full-page background — sections and tiles only
  • Use texture SVGs on P-700 backgrounds — N-100 sections only
  • Use more than two accent families on a single surface without a neutral separator
  • Introduce any color outside the six locked families
  • Use pure #000000 or #ffffff — always N-900 and N-100
Typography

DO

  • Pair EB Garamond upright with EB Garamond italic in headings — core brand typographic move
  • Use Playfair Display for subheadings, captions, button labels, UI labels, and ghosted background text
  • Use the Playfair Display ghosted text technique on N-100 sections only
  • Use Work Sans 300 for all body copy
  • Bold Work Sans within body copy for emphasis only — sparingly

DON'T

  • Use EB Garamond for body copy, ghosted backgrounds, or anything outside headings and card titles
  • Use Work Sans for headings or subheadings
  • Use bold as a subheading substitute — give it a proper heading level
  • Use uppercase on EB Garamond
  • Mix heading fonts on a single surface
  • Use Playfair Display or EB Garamond for eyebrow labels — those use Work Sans 600 uppercase
Photography

DO

  • Use black and white as the primary brand photography mode
  • Use Kodak Portra 400 color as the secondary mode
  • Prioritize real women in their 30s–40s — unposed, skin texture visible
  • Include men 40s–60s for TRT and wellness — natural energy and genuine happiness
  • Allow environmental context as long as the subject is primary
  • Use before/after framing where clinically appropriate — with L-bracket frame treatment

DON'T

  • Use measuring tape, scale, or body-measurement props
  • Use flat studio white-box lighting for brand photography
  • Airbrush or smooth skin — authenticity is intentional
  • Use stock wellness poses — jumping, arms spread, forced smile
  • Use hard-bordered BEFORE/AFTER blocks with old typography
  • Use bright saturated color palettes
Layout

DO

  • Use texture SVGs rotated 90° filling the full section — N-100 only, one per section
  • Use the logo mark PNG as a large watermark — 5–10% opacity, once per page
  • Use Playfair Display ghosted background text on N-100 sections
  • Use the L-bracket gold frame for before/after and featured content panels
  • Annotate features with callout lines in NV-700 or NV-500
  • Use NV-500 and NV-700 for all section dividers, rules, and underlines

DON'T

  • Use gradient tiles as full-section or full-page backgrounds
  • Use texture SVGs on P-700 backgrounds
  • Stack more than two different gradient tile families without a neutral between them
  • Center-align body copy over two lines
  • Apply a texture to two adjacent sections
Ad Creative

DO

  • Carry B&W photography into performance ads — the brand's most distinctive visual move
  • Apply the new type system to all new ad production
  • Use the before/after L-bracket treatment with P-700 badge labels on result ads
  • Keep legal/disclaimer copy in Playfair Display 400 caption size, bottom of frame
  • Use B&W annotated photography with NV-700/NV-500 callout lines as a primary ad layout

DON'T

  • Use the legacy Poppins all-caps type system in any new ad production
  • Use hard-bordered BEFORE/AFTER stock blocks with old typography
  • Use imagery that contradicts the photography brief
